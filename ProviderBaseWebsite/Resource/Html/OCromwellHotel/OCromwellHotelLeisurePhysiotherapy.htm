What Is Physiotherapy?
Physiotherapy is the in depth study of the body's anatomy and physiology and of all the detailed components required in movement.
Combined with an in-depth knowledge in pathology a physiotherapist can evaluate pain and dysfunction and determine how best to resolve it.
Physiotherapy is an evolving profession that combines medical knowledge with a holistic approach. Communication, problem solving approaches and autonomy are at the very core of the physiotherapy profession.
Chartered physiotherapists are governed by a professional body; The Chartered Society of Physiotherapy. This is to ensure that high standards of practice are maintained. With this accreditation physiotherapists can provide a range of treatment techniques to restore pain free movement and function in the body.
